About me

Short Bio and Current Bearings

Greetings!


Well, this is me: A GIS Analyst, former Archaeologist, and Front-End developer.
Four years on, I turned to Web Development projects, getting acquainted with front-end (HTML, CSS, JS, Bootstrap, Handlebars) and some back-end (SQL, MariaDB, MySQL, NodeJS) technologies. These provided me with a general new perpective of the GIS field and new possibilities for GIS development. For a "classic" GIS guy, all these new tech and languages, and the web and mobile platforms was overwhelming! However, I did my homework and, step by step, I have been acquiring a new array of tech and skills that comprise the core of my current professional stack. Thus, from typical desktop sotware, today I navigate along handling open data sources, APIs, and web mapping libraries to provide geospatial capabilities and data to web and mobile apps.


Before that I have been on the Archaeologist track for about twenty years. My interest on IT tech was already there, since Archaeological research moves right behind cutting-edge scientific research tech and methods. This is how I ended on Landscape Analysis and GIS in Archaeology. From there I got involved with Data Management, Databases and SQL. The resulting profile was a blend of humanistic perspective with technological skills focused on spatial-related solutions for research questions. Thanks to that I have worked in a number of spatial-related projects, both in the academic world, and in engineering and building companies. In sum, I can proudly say that I had a rich and productive career, crowned by a PhD in Archaeology.


But times change and GIS career evolved accordingly. Traditional GIS had its appeal, but reality nowadays is that tech is everywhere and new programs, procedures, workflows are the mainstream trunk of Geospatial Sciences: APIs, Sotfware Libraries, Web and Mobile Development, IDE sources, and a long etc, are now the working ground for GIS professionals, and my everyday ecosystem.
Well, this is my story. Make your own impression by navigating this website. And, if you think I can help you with any question, please, do not hesitate to contact me.
Again, thanks for coming by and best wishes!
